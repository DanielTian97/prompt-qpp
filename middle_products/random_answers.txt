---QUERY---1110199	what is wifi vs bluetooth
 WiFi and Bluetooth are both wireless technologies used for communication, but they differ in their purpose, range, and usage.

WiFi is a type of wireless networking technology that allows devices to connect to the internet or communicate with each other over short distances (typically within a few hundred feet). It uses radio waves to transmit data between devices and requires an access point (AP) or router to facilitate communication. WiFi is commonly used for internet browsing, file sharing, and online gaming.

Bluetooth, on the other hand, is a wireless personal area network (PAN) technology that allows devices to communicate with each other over short distances (typically within 30 feet). It uses radio waves to transmit data between devices and does not require an AP or router. Bluetooth is commonly used for wireless headsets, file transfer, and device pairing.

In summary, WiFi is designed for longer-range communication and is typically used for internet access, while Bluetooth is designed for shorter-range communication and is typically used for device-to-device communication.
